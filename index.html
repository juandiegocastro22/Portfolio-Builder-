What does P/E ratio mean and is Apple's good right now?
P/E ratio (Price-to-Earnings) tells you how much investors are paying per $1 of a company's profit. Think of it like a price tag — a higher number means people expect strong future growth.

Apple's current P/E is 28.4×, slightly above its 5-year average of 26×. Analysts consider it fairly valued — not cheap, but justified by its ecosystem and services growth. AI signal: BUY with a 12-month target of $240.
Should I add more AAPL to my portfolio?
You already have 25% in AAPL — that's a meaningful concentration. Before adding more, consider your risk tolerance. If you're comfortable, dollar-cost averaging makes sense here. If you want diversification, I'd suggest exploring VGT (tech ETF) to spread the risk while staying in the sector.
